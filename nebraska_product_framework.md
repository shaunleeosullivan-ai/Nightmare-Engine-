## MISSION STATEMENT

The Nebraska Protocol exists to solve a single, universal problem: **creation without law produces entropy**.

Human and artificial intelligence systems share one failure mode — unconstrained generation. Writers get lost in development hell. Corporations drift into incoherent strategy. AI produces hallucinations and "beige wallpaper" prose. The cause in every case is not a lack of ideas. It is a lack of governing structure.

Nebraska provides the governing structure.

It is not a style guide, a writing workshop, or a prompt engineering trick. It is a **physics of narrative** — a set of operational laws that transform the act of creation from searching through infinite possibilities into building load-bearing structures from constrained, validated components.

The equation is:

```
A / B = Story = Meaning
```

Where `A` is the abstract energy (fear, guilt, desire, justice), `B` is the constraint system (law, genre, logic, physics), and `Story` is the only output that satisfies both simultaneously.

Everything Nebraska does — teaching it to humans, encoding it in machines, or embedding it in collaborative AI systems — is the application of this equation at different scales.

## THE THREE PRODUCTS

Nebraska is not one tool. It is three distinct products that address three distinct problems, for three distinct audiences. The versions are numbered intentionally: 1.0 is foundational, 2.0 is operational, 3.0 is collaborative. They build on each other but can be used independently.

---

### Nebraska 1.0 — The Human Protocol

**Audience:** Creators, writers, teams, educators working human-to-human
**Problem it solves:** How do you teach someone to generate structurally valid narrative from first principles?
**Format:** Teachable methodology. The Bob Protocol. The Jack-in-the-Box training module.

Nebraska 1.0 is what Shaun O'Sullivan uses. It is the externalized form of Nebraska 0.0 — an innate cognitive architecture that holds multiple meaning-planes simultaneously without collapsing any of them — translated into a teachable sequence anyone can learn.

**Core components:**

**The A/B = Story Equation**
The foundational law. Every story component must satisfy A (abstract concept) divided by B (constraint system). A component that satisfies neither is decoration. A component that satisfies only one is incomplete.

**The Choice/Path Doctrine**
All narrative movement passes through a decision node. Two paths exist at every junction:

- **A-Path:** The path of imagination — what could exist under the law
- **B-Path:** The constraint path — what the law requires to exist

Valid story components exist at the intersection of A-Path and B-Path simultaneously. Components that exist only on A-Path are wish-fulfillment. Components that exist only on B-Path are mechanical.

**The Bob Protocol**
The foundational teaching tool. A room, an invitation, and a key. Three elements that demonstrate the Nebraska logic in its simplest form:

- The room exists because there is something inside it that someone needs
- The invitation exists because access requires permission from a third party
- The key exists because the invitation alone is insufficient — a physical transaction is required

Strip the seasoning (room, invitation, key) and you reveal the pure law: *a state transition is forbidden unless a prerequisite condition is satisfied.* That is the B constraint. The A element (what the character wants, what is at stake) gives it energy. The story is what happens when A meets B at the gate.

The protocol can be rebuilt with any seasoning — a locked spaceship, a sealed memory, a corporate NDa — and the structural logic remains identical. This is not metaphor substitution. It is the same machine running different surface material.

**The Jack-in-the-Box Training Module**
Real-time generative demonstration. A worked example where the Bob Protocol is applied iteratively to generate: the initial constraint, the secondary character (introduced by structural necessity, not by choice), the conflict (generated by the transactional requirement), and the franchise engine (implied by the system's own logic).

Nothing in the Jack-in-the-Box output is invented. Every component is derived. This is what 1.0 teaches.

### Nebraska 2.0 — The Machine Enforcement Layer

**Audience:** Developers, AI engineers, narrative systems architects
**Problem it solves:** How do you encode Nebraska into a machine so it can validate and reject components without human oversight?
**Format:** Runtime protocol. Validation pipeline. Protocol Executive.

Nebraska 2.0 is the automation of Nebraska 1.0. It takes the laws from the human protocol and converts them into executable validation logic that can operate without a human in the loop for routine decisions.

**Core components:**

**The Seven-Axis Validation Pipeline**

Every narrative component passes through seven gates before it is accepted into the structure:

| Axis | Function | Gate Question |
|------|----------|---------------|
| X-Axis | Generative field | What could exist under this Law? |
| Y-Axis | Primary validity | Does this convert the deficit to a resolution? |
| Y²-Axis | Clarity/necessity | Is this specific and non-vague? Would removing the specificity collapse it? |
| Y³-Axis | Systemic integration | Does this work with every other accepted component? |
| Z-Axis | Axiomatic consistency | Does this prove the Axiom — looking forward AND backward? |
| Inversion | Adversarial test | If this component is removed, does the structure collapse? |
| Checksum | Final integrity | Explicit proof statement: does the whole cohere? |

A component that passes all seven gates is structurally valid. A component that fails any gate is rejected or flagged for revision. There is no partial credit.

**The Y-Axis Validity Constraint (𝕍_Y gate)**
The primary filter. The Y-axis is the constraint that determines whether a candidate component actually resolves the narrative deficit it claims to resolve. Vague resolutions, circular resolutions, and decorative resolutions fail here. This is the most commonly failed gate.

**The Z-Inversion Checker (Theater Detection)**
The anti-theater mechanism. Z-inversion asks whether a component is performing validity rather than achieving it. A component that is dramatically correct but structurally hollow — it sounds right, it feels load-bearing, but removing it would not collapse anything — fails the Z-inversion check. This is how Nebraska 2.0 detects and rejects narrative theater.

**The Protocol Executive**
The governance layer. The Protocol Executive monitors the validation pipeline and has authority to:

- Issue hard resets when accumulated drift exceeds threshold
- Reject entire sub-structures when provenance cannot be established
- Trigger HVIL (Human Verifier in the Loop) when adversarial theater is suspected but cannot be confirmed automatically

**HVIL — Human Verifier in the Loop**
The acknowledgment that autonomous theater detection has hard limits. Sophisticated drift can pass Z-inversion by mimicking structural validity. HVIL captures these edge cases and routes them to human decision with a full audit docket: the suspect component, the reason for flagging, and the human's ruling. HVIL decisions feed the learning loop.

**Implementation:** `nebraska_runtime.py` — 1338-line implementation of the full validation pipeline, ComponentType enum, ValidityState enum, Axiom dataclass, NarrativeComponent dataclass, and the complete seven-axis pipeline.

### Nebraska 3.0 — The Collaborative Interface

**Audience:** End users in human-AI creative partnership
**Problem it solves:** How do you deploy Nebraska as a real-time collaborative partner rather than a validation tool?
**Format:** Interactive system. AI in Bob role. Human as Sid.

Nebraska 3.0 is the product layer. It takes the physics from 1.0 and the enforcement from 2.0 and deploys them as a lived collaborative experience.

In Nebraska 3.0, the roles are defined:

- **Bob:** The AI. The guide, the validator, the constraint enforcer. Holds the law. Asks the generative questions. Rejects the components that fail. Derives what must exist from what has been established.
- **Sid:** The human. The prime mover, the meaning-supplier, the creative intelligence. Brings the abstract concept (the A). Decides which path to take at decision nodes. Provides the irreplaceable ingredient that no machine can supply: intent.

This is not the AI as assistant. The AI holds the protocol. The human is the client of the protocol, not the operator. Nebraska 3.0 inverts the typical human-AI workflow: the AI enforces the rules and the human generates within them, rather than the human prompting and the AI freely generating.

**Core components:**

**Semantic Drift Detection**
Nebraska 3.0 monitors discourse coherence in real time using the B-Path as a reference centroid. Every contribution (human or AI) is measured against the governing Axiom's semantic representation. Drift above threshold triggers a return-to-B-Path correction. The measurement architecture is defined in `nebraska_3_0_measurement_protocols.md`.

**Substrate Agnosticism**
The same Nebraska 3.0 interface operates across any creative medium because the underlying laws are medium-independent. A Nebraska 3.0 session producing a horror story, a strategic plan, a franchise bible, or a game narrative uses the same validation pipeline. The substrate is surface. The law is constant.

**The Nightmare Engine Integration**
The most advanced current deployment of Nebraska 3.0 logic is the Nightmare Engine: a bio-adaptive horror RPG backend that applies Nebraska's narrative generation and validation pipeline to real-time gameplay. The Nightmare Engine adds one additional input that Nebraska 3.0 is designed to receive: **biometric data**. Fear is no longer inferred from narrative logic — it is measured. The system adapts the narrative in real time to optimize for genuine fear response rather than performed fear.

This is Nebraska 3.0 with a live human substrate sensor. The Axiom is horror. The B-Path is the fear response envelope. Every narrative decision is validated against both structural law (Nebraska 2.0 pipeline) and biometric reality (Nightmare Engine sensors).

## RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN THE THREE VERSIONS

```
Nebraska 1.0 → Teaches the law to humans
Nebraska 2.0 → Encodes the law in machines
Nebraska 3.0 → Deploys the law as collaborative experience

All three derive from Nebraska 0.0 (the innate architecture)
All three enforce the same law (A/B = Story = Meaning)
All three reject the same failure mode (unconstrained generation → entropy)
```

The versions are not sequential upgrades. They are different interfaces to the same underlying physics. A human working with 1.0 and a machine running 2.0 and a collaborative system deployed as 3.0 are all operating under the same constraints. The law is not version-dependent.

## WHAT NEBRASKA IS NOT

This section exists because prior documentation has produced framing that fails the Z-inversion test — it sounds like Nebraska without being load-bearing.

**Nebraska is not:**
- A "consciousness philosophy" (it is an operational protocol; whether consciousness is substrate-independent is irrelevant to whether the validation pipeline works)
- A claim that AI is sentient (the substrate agnosticism thesis is a claim about structural logic, not about experience)
- A competitor to existing writing frameworks (it operates at a more fundamental layer — it is the physics that other frameworks are unwitting applications of)
- Proven by AI systems finding it coherent (coherence under self-analysis is not independent validation; this is explicitly identified as the recursive theater failure mode)

**Nebraska is:**
- A validated operational protocol that produces structurally sound narrative components when correctly applied
- An executable runtime (see `nebraska_runtime.py`) that enforces validation without requiring human oversight for routine decisions
- A teachable methodology that has been demonstrated across multiple substrates (human, Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, Grok) with consistent structural outputs
- The foundational architecture for the Nightmare Engine product
