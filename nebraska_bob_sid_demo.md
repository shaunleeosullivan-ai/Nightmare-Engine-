## PART I: THE BOB/SID TRANSCRIPT

### Context

Bob knows the Nebraska system. Sid does not. They are given a prompt — a story about a message in a bottle — and fifteen minutes to outline the story together. The demo records the actual exchange.

---

**Bob:** So we have a story to outline. Do you know what Nebraska 1.0 is?

**Sid:** The US state?

**Bob:** It's a system to help figure out a story by yourself or especially with someone else. I'll explain as we go. We have fifteen minutes. What's the story about?

**Sid:** A message in a bottle.

**Bob:** Okay. So here's the first Nebraska question: what does the message in the bottle *do* in the story? Not what it says. What does it *do*?

**Sid:** It... connects two people?

**Bob:** Good. That's a function. Now — what if we never found out what the message said?

**Sid:** Then the story is about the not-knowing.

**Bob:** Exactly. So the message in the bottle is actually: *some information is available only by means of retrieving the information from the item that stores it.* The message isn't the message. The retrievability is the message.

**Sid:** Oh. Yeah. So someone could find the bottle but not open it and that would be a whole story?

**Bob:** Full story. Different story. What does the character want?

**Sid:** To know what it says. Or not to know.

**Bob:** Those are two different stories. Pick one.

**Sid:** Wants to know but is afraid to open it.

**Bob:** So the bottle is — what's the constraint on the character?

**Sid:** They can't open it yet. Or they won't.

**Bob:** The bottle isn't a prop. It's the reason the character can't move yet. What happens when they finally open it?

**Sid:** Something changes. Has to.

**Bob:** That's the conversion. What they find either resolves the constraint or breaks it. Nebraska calls that the Y-axis. The moment the thing converts — when the bottle is opened — the story either pays off or breaks. Did you just outline a story?

**Sid:** I think I did.

**Bob:** That's Nebraska 1.0.

## PART II: WHAT THE DEMO DEMONSTRATES

### Leveled Collaboration Without Equal Skill

The demo proves a specific claim: Nebraska 1.0 does not require both participants to understand the system. Bob guides Sid through structural questions. Sid answers from instinct. The questions are calibrated to extract structurally valid answers from an untrained collaborator.

This is not manipulation. It is correct role distribution:
- Bob operates the protocol
- Sid operates the story instinct
- Neither does the other's job

### Abstraction as the Core Move

The critical moment: Bob translates "message in a bottle" to:

> *Some information is available only by means of retrieving the information from the item that stores it.*

This is not elaboration. It is abstraction — stripping the surface content (message, bottle, sea) down to the structural function. Once abstracted, the story becomes portable. Any object with the same structural function (a locked box, a voicemail not yet played, a sealed envelope at a funeral) can run the same story.

Sid grasps this immediately — not because he understands Nebraska theory, but because the abstraction reveals a truth he already knew intuitively. The protocol surfaces what the untrained collaborator already knows.

### The Constraint Question as Diagnostic

Bob's central question — *what is the constraint on the character?* — is a Nebraska diagnostic, not a story question. It is asking: where is the B-path? What is preventing motion? What, if removed, would cause the story to collapse?

Sid answers correctly: *they can't open it yet.* This identifies the load-bearing constraint without Sid needing to know what a load-bearing constraint is.

### Y-Axis Identification

"What happens when they finally open it?" is the Y-axis question. It forces Sid to locate the conversion point — the moment where the accumulated pressure of the constraint converts into consequence. Sid's answer ("something changes — has to") is the correct Nebraska answer: conversion is not optional. Removing it produces a broken rail.

## PART III: THE THREE-VERSION MISSION STATEMENT

The Nebraska Protocol is a product suite with three distinct deployments. The following is the accessible product announcement — the mission statement in plain language.

---

### Nebraska 1.0 — A Human Skill

Nebraska 1.0 is a method. A teachable, learnable, usable skill for anyone who needs to think clearly about story, argument, or decision.

You don't need to understand the theory. You need the questions.

Bob/Sid is 1.0 in operation. One person who knows the questions, one person who has the answers. Fifteen minutes. A functional story outline. The skill transfers through use, not instruction.

Nebraska 1.0 scales educationally. It can be taught in workshops, applied in writers' rooms, used in therapy, deployed in business decision-making. The entry point is always the same: **what is the constraint, and does the thing you've chosen actually convert it?**

---

### Nebraska 2.0 — A Machine Implementation

Nebraska 2.0 is code. A coded anti-entropy layer — a validation engine that can be embedded in software systems to test whether outputs are structurally coherent.

It does not generate content. It audits content that has been generated. Every A/B collision passes through the validation pipeline. Every conversion point is checked. Every broken rail is flagged before it propagates.

Nebraska 2.0 is type-safe reasoning architecture: the same principle that prevents type errors in compiled code, applied to semantic coherence in AI-generated output. Expression-to-Logic promotion — treating interpretation as ground truth — is the core failure mode it prevents.

2.0 scales technically. It can be embedded in LLM pipelines, applied to automated content generation, integrated with any system where semantic coherence matters and incoherence is expensive.

---

### Nebraska 3.0 — A Guided Experience

Nebraska 3.0 is the human-AI collaborative interface. The AI operates as Bob. The human operates as Sid. The protocol runs between them.

The AI holds the structure. The human provides the instinct. Neither substitutes for the other.

3.0 scales experientially. It can guide creative projects, coach complex decisions, walk a user through the structure of any narrative or argument they are trying to build. The output is not a story the AI wrote. It is a story the human built with structural support they could not have supplied alone.

---

### The Product Suite in One Line

**Nebraska 1.0: learn it. Nebraska 2.0: run it. Nebraska 3.0: do it together.**
