## PART I: THE THREE PILLARS

The Nebraska Framework rests on three interconnected systems that form a complete narrative engineering architecture.

```

```

```
[INPUT: Story Idea]
     ↓

     ↓

     ↓

     ↓
[META-EVALUATE Z-Axis]→ "Does this prove the Axiom? Forward AND backward coherence?"
     ↓

     ↓
[CHECKSUM]            → "Write the explicit proof statement"
     ↓
[OUTPUT: Validated Narrative Machine]
```

### The Universal Validity Question

For any proposed component, require answers to:
1. **X-state:** What specific deficit does this address? (5 words or fewer)
2. **Y-state:** What specific resolution does it produce? (5 words or fewer)
3. **Axiom alignment:** Does this X→Y serve the governing Law?
4. **Forward coherence:** Is this the necessary next step from prior state?
5. **Inverse coherence:** Is this still necessary when viewed from the endpoint backward?

If any answer is "unclear" or "none" — the component is rejected.

## PART IV: PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS

### For Writers

**Scene Diagnostics — Why does this scene feel "off"?**

Apply the five validity questions to the scene. The first question that produces "unclear" identifies the structural fault:
- Unclear X-state → the scene lacks a defined problem
- Unclear Y-state → the scene has no resolution to offer
- Axiom misalignment → the scene is technically correct but thematically hollow
- Failed forward coherence → the scene doesn't follow logically from prior events
- Failed inverse coherence → the scene leads nowhere the story needs to go

**Outline Validation — Ensuring acts aren't decorative:**

Each act must answer: "What does this act convert, and under which Axiom?"
- Act 1: X-state established, Axiom declared
- Act 2: Conversion attempted, failed, escalated
- Act 3: Conversion completed, Axiom proven or disproven

If any act cannot state its X-state and Y-state explicitly, it is structural decoration.

### For Game Designers

**Quest Validation:**

Each quest is an X→Y conversion unit. The quest is valid only if:
- The X-state (world deficit) is established before the quest begins
- The Y-state (world resolution) is impossible without player action
- The Axiom of the game world demands this specific conversion

**Branching Narrative Coherence:**

Every branch must maintain Axiom consistency. A branch that resolves the story's central deficit in a way that violates the governing law is not a "bad ending" — it is a structural failure. The question is not "does the player win?" but "does the Axiom hold?"

### For Researchers and Educators

**Narrative Pattern Analysis:**

Any story can be analyzed by:
1. Extracting the governing Axiom (what single law does this story prove?)
2. Mapping all components to X-states and Y-states
3. Calculating coherence force for each X→Y conversion
4. Measuring the efficiency of the pedagogical payload (P = V/C)

High P-scores indicate efficient knowledge transfer. Low P-scores indicate the story is delivering content without corresponding understanding change in the reader.
