**Detection:** The transgression was detectable via the Axiom test: if Raskolnikov *succeeded* in escaping via mathematics, the Axiom "awareness of constraints enables navigation" would have been violated. His escape would have been a non-awareness-based solution — engineering around the constraint, not understanding it.

**Intervention:** Authorial correction (A1) closed the breach before the false resolution became permanent.

**Lesson for NGS 3.0 implementation:** The Z-axis inverse pass (looking from the endpoint backward) is the mechanism that catches this class of failure. The endpoint of the story — Raskolnikov riding the loop with awareness — is incompatible with "Raskolnikov escaped via math." The inverse check identifies the transgression even when the forward progression appears locally valid.
